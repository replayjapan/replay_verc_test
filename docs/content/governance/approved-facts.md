### 2. Adapted
Based on real source material, but repositioned for rePlay brand.

Approved adapted content:
- Service pages adapted from real Coopervise services
- Service copy may be reframed for rePlay positioning
- Adaptation must remain faithful to the underlying real service truth
- Do not fabricate capabilities, client outcomes, certifications, or team scale

### 3. Illustrative
Describes plausible future potential, not current operations.

Approved illustrative content:
- Domain descriptions
- Domain use cases
- Domain positioning
- Domain strategic value explanations

Illustrative content may describe:
- what a domain could become
- what type of buyer it suits
- what kind of brand, service, or media property could be built on it
- why the name is strong in the Japanese market

Illustrative content may **not** imply:
- an active business currently exists on the domain
- current traffic
- current leads or buyer interest
- verified market demand
- verified search volume
- current partnerships
- current rankings or SEO performance
- legal clearance or trademark safety unless separately confirmed

### 4. Placeholder
Used for seed/design review only. Not production-final editorial content.

Approved placeholder content:
- Videos section items
- YouTube URLs and Japanese descriptions seeded for design review

Placeholder rule:
- Videos are **not** to be presented as original rePlay-produced content
- Videos exist to support layout/design review and can be swapped later
- Do not write copy implying rePlay created, published, or owns the placeholder video content unless Craig explicitly says so

### 5. Seed-Only / Legal Review Required
Good enough for seed/design review, not approved for production launch.

Approved seed-only content:
- Privacy page

Privacy rule:
- Privacy content may be seeded for layout and review
- Privacy content must receive legal review before production launch
- Do not present seeded privacy copy as final legal advice or final legal text

- deter lowball inquiries

- support negotiation with qualified buyers

## Domain Writing Constraints

These are truth constraints, not style preferences.

Approved:
- describe plausible future use cases
- identify likely buyer or operator type
- explain why the name is strong in context
- connect domains in the same set through a shared narrative

Not approved:
- claims about current operation
- claims about current audience
- claims about current traffic or performance
- claims of verified demand without approved data
- guaranteed SEO outcomes
- exaggerated scarcity language
- invented commercial proof

### Set behavior
Domains in the same set should share a common narrative thread, but each domain still needs independent positioning for its own specific role.

Approved example pattern:
- Set thesis explains the broader ecosystem
- Individual domain copy explains the specific use case of that one domain

Not approved:
- near-duplicate descriptions across set members
- generic copy that could fit any domain equally

## Quick Review Checklist

Before approving any generated content, check:

1. Does this imply a real active business where only illustrative potential is approved?
2. Does this imply a larger company scale than reality?
3. Does this invent results, demand, traffic, or proof?
4. Does this accidentally present placeholder video content as rePlay-owned content?
5. Does this accidentally treat privacy copy as production-final?
6. Does this use domainer jargon or tier labels on the frontend?
7. Does this remain faithful to approved client names, service truth, and portfolio reality?
8. Is this specific to the exact domain/page, or could it fit almost anything?
9. Does this position rePlay as a domain marketplace instead of a development/marketing firm?
10. Is the founding year correct (December 2021)?
11. Is the brand rendered as "rePlay" (not REPLAY, Replay, or replay)?

If any answer is "yes" to 1–6 or 9, revise before use.
