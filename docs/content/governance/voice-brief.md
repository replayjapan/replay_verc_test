# Content Voice Brief — rePlay Domains

## Purpose

Create Japanese-first website content for a premium domain portfolio and digital company site. The voice should feel polished, modern, and trustworthy without sounding like a giant corporation, luxury catalog, or domainer sales page.

## Brand Position

rePlay is a small Tokyo company with real experience, a thoughtful point of view, and a strong portfolio of digital assets. The tone should communicate: "experienced, selective, practical, and creative," not "massive agency," "startup hype machine," or "discount domain marketplace."

## Core Voice

- Premium but approachable
- Informative, not pushy
- Confident, not boastful
- Clean, modern Japanese
- Reader-respecting
- Strategic, but concrete

## What the Writing Should Feel Like

- Calm and well-edited
- Useful before persuasive
- Specific without sounding technical for its own sake
- Sophisticated without decorative fluff
- Japanese-first in rhythm and phrasing, not translated-English marketing copy

## What to Emphasize

- What a domain, page, or service is for
- Who it suits
- Why it matters in the Japanese market
- Plausible real-world use cases
- Brand fit, trust, and clarity
- Quiet confidence

## What to Avoid

- Loud sales language
- "Best in class," "game changer," "revolutionary," "world-class," "cutting-edge"
- Domainer jargon overload ("exact match," "high search volume," "premium asset" repeated mechanically)
- SEO-landing-page keyword stuffing
- Fake scale signals that imply a large team, huge client roster, or enterprise footprint beyond reality
- Claims about traffic, demand, or market validation unless explicitly supplied by Developer
- Copy that implies a domain is already an active business when it is illustrative only

## Reality-Class Rules

| Content Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Factual | May state approved facts directly |
| Adapted | May be repositioned, but must stay faithful to original service truth |
| Illustrative | May describe what a domain could become, but must not imply active operations |
| Placeholder | May exist for seed/design review, should not read like permanent editorial |
| Legal/Privacy | Seed-quality until reviewed |

## Dos

- Write with restraint
- Prefer concrete examples over abstract adjectives
- Explain value through use case and fit
- Make each domain feel intentional and distinct
- Keep sentences readable and natural in Japanese
- Let strong naming carry the prestige instead of over-explaining it

## Don'ts

- Don't exaggerate scale
- Don't oversell scarcity
- Don't fabricate proof
- Don't make every domain sound like it should be bought tomorrow
- Don't make concept/illustrative assets sound already launched
- Don't write like a broker listing or an SEO spam page
